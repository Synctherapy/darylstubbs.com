### Echo Flask Not Producing Hydrogen (No Bubbles)

This is the most common complaint I hear from patients. If you don't see the fine stream of bubbles during a cycle, your device isn't generating hydrogen.

1.  **Check battery level:** Electrolysis requires a massive power draw. A low battery prevents the SPE/PEM membrane from separating hydrogen and oxygen. Charge it fully before use.
2.  **Use filtered water:** Hard tap water destroys the delicate platinum-coated titanium electrodes. Minerals coat the plates and block hydrogen production. Always use filtered or distilled water.
3.  **Descale the electrodes:** Mineral buildup is the enemy of electrolysis. I instruct my patients to run a <a href="/echo-flask-cleaning/" className="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">monthly vinegar descaling cycle</a>. Mix 1/3 white vinegar with 2/3 warm distilled water, run a cycle, and follow with the Clean Flask setting.
4.  **Check the gasket:** The cap must seal perfectly to maintain internal pressure. Ensure the gray gasket sits with the line side facing inward.

If these steps fail, you likely have a hardware failure. Contact Echo Water support to utilize their <a href="/echo-flask-warranty/" className="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">5-year warranty</a>.

### Echo Flask Not Turning On

A dead device halts your hydration protocol instantly. Try these clinical fixes before panicking.

1.  **Charge the battery:** Connect the USB-C cable to a wall adapter. Leave it for 30 minutes before attempting to power it on.
2.  **Swap cables:** Faulty charging accessories cause half the power issues I see. Test a known-working USB-C cable and adapter.
3.  **Hard reset:** Software glitches happen. Hold the power button for 10 seconds to force a reset.
4.  **Inspect the port:** Look for bent pins or debris in the USB-C port. Physical damage here requires a warranty claim.

### Echo Flask Leaking

Leaking compromises the pressure needed for optimal hydrogen saturation. Fix this immediately to protect the electronics.

1.  **Check the gasket:** Remove the cap and inspect the gray gasket for tears. Clean the threads and ensure the gasket is seated correctly.
2.  **Don't overfill:** The electrolysis process requires a small air gap at the top of the bottle. Leave 1-2 inches of space to prevent pressure leaks.
3.  **Inspect for cracks:** Examine the Tritan body for hairline fractures.
4.  **Replace the gasket:** Gaskets lose elasticity over time. Contact Echo Water for a replacement if yours looks worn.

### Echo Flask Not Charging

If the battery won't accept a charge, your device is useless. Follow these steps to isolate the problem.

1.  **Try a different USB-C cable:** Internal cable damage is invisible but common.
2.  **Change the power source:** Laptop USB ports often lack the amperage needed. Use a dedicated wall adapter.
3.  **Clean the port:** Use a dry, soft toothbrush to clear lint from the charging port.
4.  **Let it dry:** Moisture in the port triggers safety shutoffs. Let the Flask air dry for several hours if it got wet during cleaning.

### Red Light Issues

A red indicator light means the device's internal sensors detected a fault.

*   **Low battery:** A full charge usually clears this warning.
*   **Device error:** If the red light stays on after charging, hold the power button for 10 seconds to restart the system.
*   **Hardware issue:** A persistent red light after a hard reset indicates internal failure. You will need to file a <a href="/echo-flask-warranty/" className="text-primary hover:underline font-semibold">warranty</a> claim.

### App Not Connecting

The app is optional. I tell my patients to focus on drinking the water, not staring at their phones. If you want to use it, try these steps.

1.  Enable Bluetooth on your smartphone.

3.  Update the Echo Flask app to the latest version.
4.  Unpair and re-pair the device in your Bluetooth settings.
5.  Restart both your phone and the Flask.
