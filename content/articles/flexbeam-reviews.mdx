### The Clinical Science of Red Light Therapy

I never recommend a protocol without reviewing the clinical data. Photobiomodulation (PBM) stimulates cytochrome c oxidase inside your mitochondria. This process increases ATP (cellular energy) production, initiating a cascade of physiological benefits.

*   **Reduced Inflammation:** PBM modulates inflammatory pathways to reduce swelling.
*   **Improved Circulation:** It promotes vasodilation, delivering oxygen while removing metabolic waste.
*   **Accelerated Tissue Repair:** Enhanced cellular energy supports faster regeneration of muscle and connective tissue.

A comprehensive review published by Hamblin details these mechanisms and their impact on cellular function (<a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28748217/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow" className="study-link">Hamblin, 2017</a>). Clinical trials also demonstrate significant improvements in musculoskeletal pain control (<a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9980499/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow" className="study-link">PMC9980499</a>) and osteoarthritis pain intensity (<a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10531845/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow" className="study-link">PMC10531845</a>).

The FlexBeam utilizes 622nm red light for superficial tissue repair and 814nm near-infrared (NIR) light for deep muscle and joint penetration.
