### What Each Frequency Actually Does

Understanding the mechanism behind each frequency is critical for maximizing your clinical outcomes.

#### Standard RED/NIR — Continuous Mode

This is the foundational setting. In this mode, the panel delivers a consistent stream of 660nm red light and 850nm near-infrared (NIR) light at full intensity. The 660nm wavelength acts on the skin's surface to stimulate collagen production and reduce superficial inflammation. The 850nm NIR wavelength penetrates deeper into muscle tissue, joints, and bone.

Both wavelengths stimulate cytochrome c oxidase within the mitochondria. This activation increases ATP (adenosine triphosphate) production, boosting cellular energy and reducing oxidative stress (de Freitas & Hamblin, *IEEE J Sel Top Quantum Electron*, 2016; [PMCID: PMC5215870](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5215870/)). I recommend continuous mode for daily maintenance and post-workout recovery. My personal protocol involves 15 minutes in continuous RED+NIR mode, positioned six to eight inches from the panel.

#### 292 Hz — Pulse Recovery+ Mode

The 292 Hz mode pulses the light 292 times per second. Kala positions this frequency for anxiety reduction, improved sleep quality, chronic pain management, and wound healing. I use this Pulse Recovery+ setting most often after demanding days treating patients.

Rhythmic pulsing helps entrain the nervous system toward a regulated state. Athletes I treat who incorporate 292 Hz evening sessions consistently report better sleep architecture within the first week. They fall asleep faster and wake up more refreshed. For chronic pain, pulsed light modulates pain pathways more effectively than continuous light. I advise using 292 Hz for evening recovery sessions. Run a 10–15 minute session 2–3 hours before bed.

#### 586 Hz — Pulse Recovery+ Mode

The 586 Hz setting targets depression, anxiety, chronic pain, insomnia, and seasonal affective disorder (SAD). SAD is a significant clinical issue in my practice, especially during the dark winters in British Columbia.

This higher-frequency pulsing interacts with cellular signaling pathways differently than 292 Hz. Clinical research isolating these exact Hz values for mood is still evolving. However, my clinical observations show that specific patients respond highly favorably to this frequency. Use 586 Hz for addressing low mood during darker months or for stubborn insomnia. Always use light therapy as a complementary tool alongside professional care for clinical depression.

#### Custom Hz — Setting Your Own Frequency

The Kala Pro and Elite panels allow you to dial in a custom frequency from 3 Hz up to 64+ Hz via the digital dash. This capability is valuable for practitioners following specific photobiomodulation protocols. For most home users, the preset 292 Hz and 586 Hz frequencies are sufficient. The custom option becomes useful when you identify a specific frequency that yields superior results for your unique physiology.

### How to Change the Frequency on the Kala Pro Panel

The Kala Pro features an intuitive digital dash. Here is the exact step-by-step process I use to switch frequencies in my clinic.

1.  **Power on the panel.** Press the physical power button on the side or back.
2.  **Wake the digital dash.** Press the Power button on the screen menu.
3.  **Select your light mode.** Choose RED, NIR, or RED+NIR. I use RED+NIR for full-spectrum sessions.
4.  **Press Mode.** This opens the pulsing options.
5.  **Press Frequency.** Use the up/down arrows to scroll to 292 Hz, 586 Hz, or enter a custom value.
6.  **Confirm your selection.** Press confirm. The panel will pulse at your selected frequency.
7.  **Set your timer.** Select 10, 15, or 20 minutes. The panel automatically shuts off when finished.

The panel defaults back to continuous mode when powered off. You must reselect your pulsed mode for each session.
