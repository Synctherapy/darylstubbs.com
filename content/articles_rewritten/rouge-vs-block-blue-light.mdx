### Rouge Overview: The 8-Wavelength Powerhouse

Rouge dominates the clinical and at-home photobiomodulation space through sheer precision. Their G4 series represents the most advanced hardware I evaluate in my practice. The standout feature is their industry-leading 8-wavelength system.

This spectrum covers superficial red light (630-670nm) and deep-penetrating near-infrared (810-1060nm). In my clinical practice, targeted photobiomodulation dictates recovery speed. Different light frequencies stimulate distinct cellular responses across various tissue depths.

Shorter red wavelengths (630-670nm) stimulate collagen production and reduce surface inflammation. Patients often tell me they see rapid improvements in skin texture and faster wound healing. 

The near-infrared spectrum (810-1060nm) is where Rouge truly separates itself from competitors. The unique 1060nm wavelength penetrates deep into muscle tissue, joints, and bone. I use this specific frequency to reduce chronic pain and accelerate post-exercise muscle recovery.

Athletes in my care experience significantly reduced delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS) when using Rouge panels. The 1060nm wavelength provides deep tissue benefits that standard 850nm panels simply miss.

Rouge also offers granular control through an intuitive mobile app. You can adjust all 8 wavelengths independently and set precise pulsing frequencies from 1 to 10,000 Hz. When designing a protocol for a patient with osteoarthritis, this level of customization is mandatory.
